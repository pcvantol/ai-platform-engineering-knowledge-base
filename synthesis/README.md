# Knowledge Synthesis Framework

This area defines the canonical framework for combining existing knowledge into higher-level engineering understanding.

The Knowledge Base remains canonical. Certified Knowledge remains authoritative. Synthesis creates derived understanding and never changes Certified Knowledge.

This framework does not generate synthesized knowledge, create publications, implement AI synthesis, define storage formats, or modify existing Certified Knowledge.

## Core Principle

Knowledge is synthesized.

Knowledge is never rewritten during synthesis.

Knowledge is never permanently merged during synthesis.

Synthesis produces derived artifacts that reference canonical knowledge.

## Synthesis Objectives

The framework supports synthesis for:

- engineering analysis
- architecture reasoning
- design trade-offs
- engineering recommendations
- publication generation
- executive summaries
- engineering reviews
- roadmap generation
- capability analysis
- engineering research

Synthesis helps humans and AI systems understand relationships across knowledge. It does not create new Certified Knowledge by itself.

## Synthesis Inputs

Canonical synthesis inputs may include:

- Certified Knowledge
- Generalized Knowledge
- Knowledge Concepts
- Engineering Domains
- Knowledge Collections
- multiple repositories
- publications as reference-only inputs
- evidence

Input authority must remain visible. Certified Knowledge is authoritative. Publications are derived references. Evidence supports traceability but is not knowledge by itself.

## Synthesis Model

A Synthesized Artifact is a derived artifact that combines, compares, summarizes, or reasons over existing knowledge.

Every Synthesized Artifact should support:

- artifact identity
- synthesis purpose
- synthesis type
- audience
- scope
- source Knowledge Objects
- source evidence where relevant
- source publications where used as reference-only material
- derived conclusions
- confidence or uncertainty
- knowledge coverage
- identified gaps
- identified conflicts
- assumptions
- revision history
- owner or steward
- review status

A Synthesized Artifact is not a Knowledge Object. It may reference Knowledge Objects, evidence, and publications, but it does not become canonical knowledge.

## Synthesis Outputs

Supported synthesis outputs include:

- Architecture Analysis
- Engineering Report
- Capability Assessment
- Research Summary
- Decision Support
- Best Practices
- Engineering Comparison
- Cross-domain Insight
- Publication Draft
- Executive Summary

Outputs are always derived. They never replace Certified Knowledge, Generalized Knowledge, Knowledge Concepts, or lifecycle artifacts.

## Synthesis Flow

```text
Synthesis Intent
->
Query and Retrieval
->
Source Knowledge Selection
->
Relationship and Evidence Analysis
->
Conflict and Gap Identification
->
Derived Understanding
->
Synthesized Artifact
```

Synthesis depends on retrieval, but retrieval and synthesis are different activities. Retrieval exposes existing knowledge. Synthesis creates derived understanding from retrieved knowledge.

## Traceability Model

Every synthesized artifact must remain fully traceable.

Canonical synthesis traceability:

```text
Synthesized Output
->
Certified Knowledge
->
Generalized Knowledge
->
Knowledge Concepts
->
Knowledge Candidates
->
Engineering Observations
->
Knowledge Sources
->
Repositories
```

Traceability requirements:

- Synthesized outputs must identify all source Knowledge Objects.
- Synthesized conclusions must point to supporting Certified Knowledge whenever they claim canonical authority.
- Supporting evidence must remain distinguishable from knowledge.
- Publications used during synthesis must be marked as reference-only.
- AI-generated synthesis must identify uncertainty and assumptions.
- Unsupported conclusions must not appear authoritative.

## AI Synthesis Principles

AI may:

- combine knowledge
- identify patterns
- summarize
- compare
- contrast
- identify gaps
- identify conflicts
- produce recommendations
- suggest future work
- draft synthesized artifacts

AI must not:

- create Certified Knowledge during synthesis
- change Certified Knowledge
- remove traceability
- create unsupported engineering facts
- hide uncertainty
- treat publications as canonical sources
- present synthesis output as certification

AI synthesis output must remain explainable, traceable, and reviewable by human-governed roles.

## Synthesis Safety

Every synthesized conclusion must be explainable.

Synthesis must support:

- source attribution
- supporting evidence
- confidence
- knowledge coverage
- identified uncertainty
- missing evidence
- conflicting knowledge
- assumptions

If the available knowledge does not support a conclusion, synthesis must expose the limitation rather than filling the gap with unsupported claims.

## Conflict Handling

Synthesis may encounter knowledge that disagrees.

Conflict types include:

- conflicting concepts
- multiple engineering approaches
- superseded knowledge
- alternative patterns
- competing recommendations
- inconsistent evidence

Synthesis must expose conflicts rather than hide them.

Conflict handling should:

- identify the conflicting Knowledge Objects
- preserve each object's lifecycle state and certification state
- distinguish active Certified Knowledge from superseded, deprecated, or retired knowledge
- explain the context where each approach applies
- identify missing evidence where resolution is not possible
- recommend review or promotion work when appropriate

Synthesis does not resolve conflicts by modifying Certified Knowledge. Conflict resolution that affects canonical knowledge must follow review, promotion, and certification governance.

## Publication Support

Knowledge synthesis may generate publication drafts.

Publication-oriented synthesis may:

- organize Certified Knowledge for an audience
- summarize related Certified Knowledge
- compare multiple certified patterns or practices
- prepare executive summaries
- draft architecture or engineering guidance
- identify publication scope and source coverage

Publication generation must reference Certified Knowledge. Publications remain derived artifacts and must follow the Publication Framework.

## Governance

Synthesis governance ensures that derived understanding remains explainable, traceable, and clearly separated from canonical knowledge.

Governance expectations:

- Synthesis must identify source knowledge and source authority.
- Synthesis must preserve lifecycle and certification context.
- Synthesis must distinguish conclusions, assumptions, uncertainty, and evidence.
- Synthesis must not modify Knowledge Objects.
- Synthesis outputs that influence publications must follow publication governance.
- Synthesis outputs that propose new or changed knowledge must enter the lifecycle as candidates or review proposals.

## Quality Expectations

Synthesis should maximize:

- engineering correctness
- traceability
- clarity
- reusability
- consistency
- explainability
- knowledge coverage

Synthesis should minimize:

- hallucination
- unsupported conclusions
- knowledge duplication
- loss of context
- hidden conflicts
- stale publication dependency

## Scalability Principles

The framework must support synthesis across:

- multiple repositories
- multiple engineering domains
- large knowledge collections
- future AI systems
- future publication systems

Synthesis must scale without requiring repository redesign, knowledge duplication, or changes to Certified Knowledge.

## Integration With Other Frameworks

Synthesis depends on the Knowledge Query & Retrieval Framework to select and navigate source knowledge.

Synthesis depends on the Knowledge Model for identity, relationships, metadata, traceability, certification state, and lifecycle state.

Synthesis depends on the Publication Framework when synthesized outputs become publication drafts.

Synthesis depends on the AI Knowledge Operations Framework when AI assists with synthesis.

Synthesis depends on the Review & Promotion Framework and Certification Framework when synthesized insights suggest new knowledge, revisions, conflicts, or certification changes.
